AMORTIZATION

Amortization refers to the process of gradually paying off a debt or loan over time through regular payments. Each payment typically includes both interest and a portion of the principal (the original loan amount). As payments are made, the amount allocated to the principal increases, while the portion for interest decreases. Amortization is commonly used in loans such as mortgages, car loans, and personal loans. A key feature of amortized loans is that they are paid off in full by the end of the loan term, assuming all payments are made on time. Amortization schedules, which show how each payment is divided between interest and principal, help borrowers understand how much of their payment goes toward reducing the loan balance over time.
